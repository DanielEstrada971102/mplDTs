_correct_cords(x, y, z)[source]

Correct the coordinates of the super layer. Bear in mind that the station reference frame is rotated pi/2 with respect to the CMS frame depending on the super layer number:

if SL == 1 or 3:

CMS -> x: right, y: up, z: forward, SuperLayer -> x: right, y: forward, z: down That is, a rotation matrix of -90 degrees around the x-axis.

\[\begin{split}Rx(-\pi/2) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]
if SL == 2:

CMS -> x: right, y: up, z: forward, SuperLayer -> x: backward, y: right, z: down

That is, a rotation matrix of 90 degrees around the z-axis. then a rotation of -90 degrees around the x-axis.

\[\begin{split}R_x(-\pi/2) R_z(\pi/2) = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & -1 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \cdot \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & 1 \\ -1 & 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]
Parameters:

  • x (float) – x-coordinate.

  • y (float) – y-coordinate.

  • z (float) – z-coordinate.

Corrected coordinates (x, y, z).
